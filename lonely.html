Loneliness is a widespread issue that affects people of all ages and backgrounds around the world. According to a survey by the health service provider Cigna, in 2020, before the COVID-19 pandemic, 61% of adults in the United States reported feeling lonely. The pandemic has only exacerbated this issue, with lockdowns and social distancing measures limiting social interactions. Loneliness can have serious consequences for both physical and mental health. Studies have linked loneliness to increased risk of heart disease, depression, anxiety, and even premature death. Additionally, lonely individuals may experience lower productivity, decreased job performance, and higher healthcare costs.
